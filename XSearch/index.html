Search Modes Explained

Global Search: The standard investigator mode. Searches the entirety of 𝕏 applying your selected keywords, engagement limits, and deep filters.

Profile Search: Target a specific user. Enter an 𝕏 Handle to exclusively search within their past posts, replies, and quoted content while still applying advanced rules.

Smart Search: For advanced users. Paste your own complex Boolean logic strings into the "Smart Sentence" field, and use the UI to stack additional filters on top without breaking the syntax.

Words & Syntax

Capital OR Matters: Use capitalized OR between words to find either term. Lowercase "or" will break the search engine.
Correct: dogs OR cats

Exact Phrases: Quotes force rigid matching, bypassing autocorrect entirely.
Example: "golden hour"

Forced Matches (+): Add a plus sign to force a match for deliberate typos or unbroken codes.
Example: +radiooooo

These hashtags: Finds posts that include specific hashtags natively.
Example: #OSINT

Accounts & Location

Target Lists: Restrict your search to a specific curated cohort. Incredible for filtering noise.
Example: username/list-slug

Trusted Networks: Under Users Type, selecting "Trusted" searches only second-degree connections (people followed by people you follow). Great alternative to Blue Checkmarks.

Geocode (Strict): Draw an algorithmic geofence. Often better than broad "Location".
Example: 40.71,-74.00,5km

Tags Accounts: Shows any post where usernames are tagged, regardless of origin.

Engagement & Timelines

Minimums (MIN): High thresholds wipe out bots and isolate viral consensus opinions.

Maximums (MAX): Clicking the MIN/MAX toggle to set a maximum ceiling (e.g. Max Likes: 10) is the ultimate trick for finding raw, unamplified eyewitness data during a breaking crisis.

Anchor Tweet IDs: By picking "After Tweet ID" in Time Constraints, you use the Snowflake ID embedded in a specific Tweet to perfectly boundary a search to the exact millisecond, bypassing timezone bugs.

OSINT Machine Tags (Deep Metadata):

  • Media Only (No Text): Uses lang:zxx to find raw imagery or leaked documents entirely devoid of human commentary.
  • Mentions Only (Spam): Uses lang:qam to find posts containing only @mentions. Ideal for tracking automated botnets or harassment rings.
  • Cashtags Only: Uses lang:qct to find posts consisting solely of ticker symbols (e.g. $TSLA), exposing financial pump-and-dump coordination.
  • Undefined: Uses lang:und to capture anomalous character sets, dense numerical dumps, or encrypted strings.
